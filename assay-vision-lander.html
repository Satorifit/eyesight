Will this fix my eyesight?

No — and anyone telling you a capsule will is selling you something. Formula No. 01 is a dietary supplement that supplies crocin, a carotenoid your diet almost certainly doesn't provide. It's nourishment, not correction. If your vision is changing, see an optometrist, not a supplement brand.

Should I stop taking my eye vitamin?

Not on our account. Lutein and zeaxanthin are well-studied and worth taking — this isn't a replacement for them, it's the carotenoid they leave out. Different pigment, different source, same family.

Do I keep the blue light glasses?

Sure, if you like them. They work on the light hitting your eyes; this works on what your eyes are made of. They're not competitors — they're not even doing the same job.

Why exactly 28 mg?

Because that's the daily dose used in the published human research on this extract. Bigger numbers of weaker, unstandardized material look impressive on a label but aren't what anyone studied. We match the studied dose and verify it by independent testing, every batch.

How do I know the saffron is real?

Three ways. It's a branded, standardized extract sourced through its manufacturer's authorized supply chain. Every batch carries an independent lab report, linked from your jar. And your first order includes real threads with a water-test card — check it yourself at home.

Can I take it with medication?

Ask your doctor first — especially with antidepressants or blood thinners, or if you're pregnant or nursing. Saffron is biologically active; that's the point, and anything active deserves the conversation.
